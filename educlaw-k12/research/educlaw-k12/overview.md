> **Target Market:** K-12 schools (public and private, US-focused)
> **Date:** 2026-03-05

## 1. Market Context

### 1.1 What K-12 Schools Are

K-12 schools serve students from Kindergarten (age 5) through 12th grade (age 18), encompassing:
- **Elementary school:** Grades K–5 (ages 5–11)
- **Middle school:** Grades 6–8 (ages 11–14)
- **High school:** Grades 9–12 (ages 14–18)

The US K-12 market includes approximately:
- **130,000+** public and private schools
- **50 million+** students
- **3.7 million** teachers

### 1.2 Why K-12 Is Distinct from Higher Ed

The base EduClaw product serves both K-12 and higher education. K-12 extensions are necessary because K-12 schools have fundamentally different regulatory and operational requirements:

| Dimension | K-12 | Higher Education |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| Student age | 5–18 (minors) | 18+ (adults) |
| FERPA rights holder | **Parents** (under 18) | **Student** themselves |
| Health oversight | School nurse required; state-mandated immunizations | Student-managed health |
| Discipline | IDEA protections for disabled students; mandatory reporting | Code of conduct, less regulated |
| Special education | Federal IDEA mandate (Free Appropriate Public Education) | ADA/Section 504 only |
| Grade progression | Explicit promotion/retention decisions | Credit accumulation |
| Guardian involvement | High (parents are primary contact) | Low (FERPA limits info sharing) |
| Mandatory reporting | Child abuse, immunization compliance, discipline to state | Research reporting only |

### 1.3 Market Segments

| Segment | Size | Pain Points |
|---------|------|------------|
| Large public school districts (10,000+ students) | Top 500 districts | Already have PowerSchool or Infinite Campus; integration challenges |
| Mid-size public districts (1,000–10,000 students) | ~5,000 districts | Aging legacy systems; compliance burden growing |
| Small/rural public districts (<1,000 students) | ~10,000 districts | Budget-constrained; often using manual systems |
| Private/independent K-12 schools | ~33,000 schools | Underserved; commercial SIS too expensive |

**EduClaw K-12 primary target:** Private/independent schools, charter schools, and small/rural districts — currently underserved by $5–20/student/year commercial systems. These schools often manage discipline in spreadsheets, health records on paper, and IEPs in Word documents.

## 2. The Four K-12 Extension Domains

### 2.1 Discipline Management

**What it is:** The process of documenting, tracking, and responding to student behavioral incidents — from minor classroom disruptions to serious safety events.

**Key entities:**
- **Incident** — The event itself (date, time, location, type, severity)
- **Involved students** — One or more students with individual roles
- **Consequences** — Actions taken: detention, suspension, expulsion, referral
- **Parent notifications** — Legally required communication to guardians
- **Referrals** — To counselors, administrators, law enforcement
- **Appeals** — Formal challenge to discipline decisions

**Current state in most K-12 schools:**
- Small schools: paper discipline referral forms, manual log books
- Mid-size schools: PowerSchool or Infinite Campus built-in module
- Alternative tracking: Google Forms + Sheets, Excel

**Why it matters:** Discipline data drives:
- PBIS (Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports) implementation
- Early warning systems for at-risk students
- State reporting (required in most states for suspensions 10+ days)
- Equity analysis (disproportionality identification)
- Legal defense in dispute situations

### 2.2 Health Records

**What it is:** Comprehensive medical information managed by the school nurse or health office to support student wellbeing and ensure regulatory compliance.

**Key entities:**
- **Health profile** — Allergies, chronic conditions, medications, physician contact
- **Office visit** — Each nurse visit with complaint, assessment, treatment, and disposition
- **Medication administration log** — Time-stamped record of each dose given
- **Immunization records** — Vaccine history, lot numbers, dates, provider
- **Immunization waivers** — Medical, religious, or philosophical exemptions
- **Emergency health information** — Allergies, EpiPen location, emergency contacts

**Current state in most K-12 schools:**
- Paper-based cumulative health files in the nurse's office
- Separate immunization tracking binders
- Magnus Health or Frontline Health for tech-forward schools
- SchoolDoc, EduHealth for dedicated school nurse EHR

**Why it matters:**
- State law requires specific immunizations before enrollment
- Untracked allergies and medications create liability
- School nurses need immediate access to health info in emergencies
- Medication administration must be documented for liability protection

### 2.3 Special Education

**What it is:** The federally mandated system (under IDEA) for identifying students with disabilities and providing them Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) through Individualized Education Programs (IEPs) or Section 504 accommodation plans.

**Key entities:**
- **Referral** — Formal request for special education evaluation
- **Evaluation** — Multidisciplinary assessment process
- **Eligibility determination** — Meeting to determine if student qualifies
- **IEP document** — Legal binding education plan with goals, services, accommodations
- **IEP goals** — Measurable academic/behavioral targets
- **IEP services** — Related services (speech therapy, OT, PT, counseling)
- **IEP team** — Parents, teachers, school psychologist, administrators, student (age 16+)
- **Progress notes** — Documented goal progress each reporting period
- **Section 504 plan** — Accommodation plan for students who don't qualify for IEP

**Current state in most K-12 schools:**
- Word documents / PDF templates for IEPs (extremely common in small schools)
- Frontline Special Education (formerly Excent) — market leader
- Infinite Campus special ed module
- State-run IEP systems (e.g., SEIS in California)
- Manual tracking of service minutes in Excel

**Why it matters:**
- IEP is a **legal document** — schools face litigation for non-compliance
- IDEA mandates strict timelines (60 days to evaluate, 30 days to write IEP)
- Annual reviews are legally required
- Triennial re-evaluations are legally required
- Service delivery must be tracked vs. IEP mandate (compliance gap reporting)
- In 2024, only 20 states fully met IDEA requirements — non-compliance is rampant

### 2.4 Grade Promotion

**What it is:** The end-of-year process of determining whether each student advances to the next grade level based on academic performance, attendance, and other criteria.

**Key entities:**
- **Promotion review** — Per-student assessment of promotion eligibility
- **Promotion criteria** — GPA, attendance, subject-specific pass requirements
- **Review team** — Principal, teachers, counselor, parents
- **Decision** — Promote, retain, or conditional promotion
- **Parent notification** — Required by most state laws before a set date (often May 1)
- **Intervention plan** — Support plan for retained/at-risk students

**Current state in most K-12 schools:**
- Manual review at end of year by teachers and principal
- No systematic software support — done in grade books and principal judgment
- Some districts use PowerSchool's GPA/attendance reports to identify at-risk students
- Promotion letters generated manually in Word

**Why it matters:**
- Grade retention is a significant decision with long-term student impact
- Parents have notification rights (typically May 1 deadline)
- Districts must document retention criteria and decisions for accountability
- Many states require formal multi-stakeholder teams for retention decisions
- Early identification (October/November) of at-risk students enables intervention

## 3. Key Industry Trends

### 3.1 PBIS Integration
The shift from punitive to positive behavioral frameworks is accelerating. PBIS (Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports) is now implemented in 26,000+ schools. Software must track positive behavior recognition alongside incidents — not just infractions.

### 3.2 Multi-Tiered Support Systems (MTSS)
Schools are moving toward MTSS frameworks that integrate academic, behavioral, and social-emotional learning data. K-12 SIS systems are increasingly expected to surface early warning indicators that cross all domains.

### 3.3 Equity and Disproportionality Monitoring
Federal requirements under IDEA (34 CFR 300.646) require states to identify significant disproportionality in the identification, placement, and discipline of students with disabilities by race/ethnicity. Software must support these equity reports.

### 3.4 Digital Health Records Momentum
Post-COVID, schools accelerated adoption of digital health management. State immunization registries now provide APIs for direct immunization data sync. HIPAA/FERPA compliance in digital health systems is increasingly scrutinized.

### 3.5 AI-Assisted Early Warning
Commercial vendors (PowerSchool, Infinite Campus) are embedding AI-based early warning systems that flag students at risk of discipline issues, grade retention, or dropout. This is a differentiator opportunity.

### 3.6 Chronic Absenteeism Focus
The post-COVID absenteeism crisis (chronic absenteeism rates doubled nationally) has elevated attendance + discipline + health data integration as a priority. The parent EduClaw already has attendance; K-12 extensions add the discipline and health dimensions.

## 4. Regulatory Landscape Summary

| Framework | Domain Impact | Detail |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) | Special Education | IEP, FAPE, LRE, evaluation timelines, parental rights |
| FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) | All domains | Education records include health, discipline, and special ed records |
| HIPAA | Health Records | Applies to covered entities; school-maintained student health records typically fall under FERPA, not HIPAA |
| Section 504 (Rehabilitation Act) | Special Ed / Health | Accommodation plans for students with disabilities that don't qualify for IDEA |
| ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) | Special Ed / Health | Applies to schools for physical accessibility and accommodation |
| State Immunization Laws | Health Records | Vary by state; define required vaccines, exemption types, and provisional enrollment |
| IDEA Part C | Special Education | Early intervention (birth–2); K-12 extension handles Part B (3–21) |
| Title IX | Discipline | Discipline records related to sexual harassment/assault have special protections |
| McKinney-Vento | Students | Homeless students have special enrollment and service rights |

## 5. Competitive Landscape Summary

| Competitor | Primary Strength | Weakness |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| PowerSchool | Market leader; discipline + health + sped modules | Expensive ($20–40/student); complex; 2023 data breach hurt trust |
| Infinite Campus | Strong IEP/504 and behavior tracking | Expensive; requires IT staff to manage |
| Frontline Education | Best-in-class IEP management; service delivery tracking | Standalone (not integrated with SIS); expensive |
| Magnus Health | Best health records UX for school nurses | Standalone; no discipline or sped integration |
| SchoolDoc | School nurse EHR | Very limited scope |
| SEIS (California) | Free state IEP system | California only; poor UX; no ERP integration |

**EduClaw K-12 opportunity:** Provide an integrated, affordable, open-source alternative that combines discipline, health, special education, and grade promotion **within the same ERP system** that also handles enrollment, grading, attendance, and fees — eliminating the 4–6 separate software subscriptions small schools currently maintain.
